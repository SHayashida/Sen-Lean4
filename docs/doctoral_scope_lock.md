## 1. Dissertation thesis

Machine-verified correctness does not by itself license generalization, repair
reporting, or institutional action. Each transition requires a distinct
abstraction contract and an associated preservation theorem.

This thesis is developed vertically through the Sen liberal-paradox program. It
is not presented as a completed theory shared by multiple theorem families.
Each chapter has its own claim boundary and evidence obligations.

## 3. Locked generality claim

Allowed:

> The dissertation develops a contract-based vertical account of how auditable
> finite evidence about Sen's liberal paradox may be connected to a general
> semantic theorem, repair reporting, and institutional use.

Forbidden:

> The dissertation completes a common finite-to-general bridge theory for
> multiple social-choice impossibility theorems.

Vertical generality means:

```text
finite evidence
-> semantic obstruction
-> general Sen theorem
-> grouped repair report
-> institutionally warranted action
```

Not all five transitions are already completed. M4 remains future work at the
time of this lock.
