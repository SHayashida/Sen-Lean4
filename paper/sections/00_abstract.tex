\begin{abstract}
We present a verification-first Possibility Atlas pipeline for computational social choice.
The contribution is not a new social-choice theorem, but a narrow chain of trust that turns SAT search into auditable evidence: Lean semantics and theorems on one side, DIMACS generation plus manifest auditing in the middle, and LRAT checked by the Lean kernel on the other.
For the fixed Sen base case ($n=2$, $m=4$), the system enumerates SAT/UNSAT regions, extracts one MUS and one small MCS candidate for boundary explanation, and validates SAT witnesses independently of solver output strings.
We state the guarantee boundary explicitly: semantic theorems and LRAT replay are proved in Lean, schema conformance is audited mechanically, and encoding correctness is claimed only up to audited sen24 invariants plus a finite check that short-cycle constraints coincide with acyclicity on four alternatives.
\end{abstract}
