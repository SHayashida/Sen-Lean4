\section{Sen24 Case Study}

The sen24 atlas is the fixed case study where the paper's trust argument becomes concrete.
It supplies two different but complementary evidence paths:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Semantic path.} \texttt{SocialChoiceAtlas/Sen/BaseCase24/Theorem.lean} proves the sen24 impossibility statement in Lean by constructing witness profiles that force 3-cycles or 4-cycles.
  \item \textbf{Artifact path.} \texttt{SocialChoiceAtlas/Sen/BaseCase24/SATSenCNF.lean} replays an LRAT certificate for the committed DIMACS baseline, proving UNSAT of the audited CNF instance in the Lean kernel.
\end{itemize}

These paths are intentionally linked but not conflated. The semantic theorem is a statement about social-choice axioms. The LRAT replay is a statement about one concrete CNF artifact. The paper's chain of trust is the argument that these two forms of evidence are compatible under an explicit audit contract.

\paragraph{Acyclicity bridge for four alternatives.}
The main sen24-specific semantic gap is that the CNF uses \texttt{NO\_CYCLE3}/\texttt{NO\_CYCLE4}, while the Lean side uses \texttt{Acyclic}.
We do not leave this implicit. The repository includes \texttt{scripts/check\_acyclicity\_short\_cycles.py}, which exhaustively checks all relations on the four-alternative domain and verifies that, under asymmetry, acyclicity is equivalent to the absence of directed 3-cycles and 4-cycles.
This justifies the sen24 encoding choice without overclaiming a general theorem for arbitrary numbers of alternatives.

For UNSAT verification, the committed reference case uses LRAT checked by Lean.
For SAT examples, the gallery records deterministic ranking and witness-validation statistics for each selected case.

\paragraph{Reproducible repair tables.}
For a fully solved atlas run (\texttt{--prune none}), axiom-level repair sets are enumerated with
\texttt{scripts/enumerate\_repairs.py}. The resulting \texttt{mcs\_all}, \texttt{mcs\_min\_size}, and \texttt{mcs\_min\_all} fields in
\texttt{atlas.json} provide inclusion-minimal SAT repairs per UNSAT case.
This is C6 evidence. It is stronger than, and deliberately separate from, the narrower C2 claim of one MUS plus one small MCS candidate.

\paragraph{Validated non-trivial SAT rule cards.}
Each selected SAT gallery entry is accompanied by \texttt{rule\_card.md} and \texttt{rule\_card.tex},
including non-triviality metrics and concrete profile witnesses. SAT assignments are checked with
\texttt{scripts/validate\_sat\_witness.py} before rule cards are emitted.

\paragraph{Triangulation and poset structure artifacts.}
The optimization-side triangulation report is generated as \texttt{repair\_triangulation.md} via
\texttt{scripts/triangulate\_repairs.py}, comparing independent optimum repair sizes against
\texttt{mcs\_min\_size}/\texttt{mcs\_min\_all}. The frontier structure is rendered as a subset-poset/Hasse graph
(\texttt{frontier\_hasse.dot} and \texttt{frontier\_hasse.png}) via \texttt{scripts/plot\_hasse\_frontier.py}.
