\section{Conclusion}

We provide a practical template for proof-carrying possibility atlases in computational social choice.
The sen24 study shows that small-scope exploration can still produce publishable systems evidence when the chain of trust is explicit: semantic statements are proved in Lean, concrete UNSAT artifacts are kernel-checked from LRAT, and the remaining encoder boundary is exposed as an audit contract rather than hidden inside solver trust.

The immediate next step is controlled expansion of the atlas while preserving the same trust and reproducibility guarantees.
