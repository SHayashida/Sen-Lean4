\section{Conclusion}

We provide a practical template for auditable possibility atlases in computational social choice.
The sen24 study shows that small-scope exploration can still produce publishable automated-reasoning evidence when the chain of trust is explicit: semantic statements are proved in Lean, committed UNSAT reference artifacts are kernel-checked from LRAT, reported SAT witnesses are validated against CNF, and the remaining encoder boundary is exposed as the audit contract of Proposition~\ref{prop:sen24-contract} rather than hidden inside solver trust.

The immediate next step is controlled expansion of the atlas while preserving the same trust and reproducibility guarantees.
