\section{Definitions}

\paragraph{Definition 1 (Lever-structured encoding).}
A lever-structured encoding is a triple \((\Lambda,\mathcal{S},\varphi)\) such that:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),leftmargin=1.8em]
\item \(\Lambda\) is a finite family of lever labels;
\item \(\mathcal{S} \subseteq 2^\Lambda\) is a family of admissible active lever sets;
\item \(\varphi : \mathcal{S} \to \mathsf{CNF}\) maps each active lever set \(S \in \mathcal{S}\) to a CNF instance \(\varphi(S)\).
\end{enumerate}
When \(S \in \mathcal{S}\) is fixed, the pair \((S,\varphi(S))\) is the encoded instance induced by that active lever set.
The theorem in this paper is about such encoded instances, not about bare encodings with no active set specified.

\paragraph{Definition 2 (Clause-multiset equivalence).}
Let \(\varphi_1(S_1)\) and \(\varphi_2(S_2)\) be two encoded CNF instances.
We write
\[
\varphi_1(S_1) \equiv_{\mathsf{CM}} \varphi_2(S_2)
\]
when the mapped comparison artifact establishes a clause-level bijection between the two clause multisets after applying the designated variable renaming map.
In this manuscript, the scope of \(\equiv_{\mathsf{CM}}\) is restricted to the mapped comparisons reported in
\code{results/20260401/candidate\_b\_minlib\_granularity/comparison.json}.
We do not use \(\equiv_{\mathsf{CM}}\) as a synonym for unrestricted logical equivalence or unrestricted semantic equivalence.

\paragraph{Definition 3 (Direct lever-level minimal repair family).}
Let \((\Lambda,\mathcal{S},\varphi)\) be a lever-structured encoding, let \(S \in \mathcal{S}\), and suppose that \(\varphi(S)\) is \(\mathsf{UNSAT}\).
The raw lever-level minimal repair family of \(\varphi(S)\) is
\[
\mathsf{Rep}^{\mathrm{raw}}_\Lambda(\varphi(S))
:=
\left\{
R \subseteq S \;\middle|\;
\begin{array}{l}
\varphi(S \setminus R)\text{ is }\mathsf{SAT}, \text{ and}\\[2pt]
\varphi(S \setminus R')\text{ is }\mathsf{UNSAT}\text{ for every }R' \subsetneq R
\end{array}
\right\}.
\]
This definition is direct at the lever level.
It does not define raw repair explanation as the image of a clause-level MCS projection.

\paragraph{Definition 4 (Raw repair explanation equivalence).}
Let \(\mathcal{R}_1 \subseteq 2^{S_1}\) and \(\mathcal{R}_2 \subseteq 2^{S_2}\) be two raw repair families over active lever sets \(S_1\) and \(S_2\).
We write
\[
\mathcal{R}_1 \equiv_{\mathsf{RRE}} \mathcal{R}_2
\]
iff there exists a bijection \(\pi : S_1 \to S_2\) such that
\[
\mathcal{R}_2 = \{\pi[R] : R \in \mathcal{R}_1\},
\qquad
\pi[R] := \{\pi(r) : r \in R\}.
\]
Thus \(\equiv_{\mathsf{RRE}}\) preserves both the number of repairs and the cardinality of each repair set.
In particular, the family whose only repair is the singleton \code{minlib}
is not \(\equiv_{\mathsf{RRE}}\)-equivalent to the family whose only repair is the two-lever set
consisting of \code{decisive\_voter0} and \code{decisive\_voter1}.

\paragraph{Grouped repair explanation.}
Grouped repair explanation is a different object from Definition~3.
It arises only after fixing an explicit abstraction contract that groups raw lever labels into presentation categories.
This paper does not claim a complete characterization of when grouped repair explanation is invariant.
Its narrower claim is that raw repair explanation can already fail to be invariant before any grouping contract is chosen.
