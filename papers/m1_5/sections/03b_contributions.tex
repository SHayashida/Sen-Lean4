\section{Contributions}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item We distinguish raw repair explanation from grouped repair explanation; the former is experimentally shown to be non-invariant under clause-equivalent encodings, while the latter requires an explicit abstraction contract.
\item We state an existence theorem grounded in the Candidate B base-case artifact bundle: in the Sen24 base case, there are clause-equivalent bundled and split encodings whose encoded UNSAT instances have different raw lever-level minimal repair families.
\item We separate finite witness legitimization from repair presentation canonicality: the former concerns whether a finite impossibility witness may be used at all, while the latter concerns how its repairs may be grouped and reported.
\item We show why the Candidate A collapse matters methodologically: once the current five-lever granularity collapses neighboring-case repair tests into size-1 repairs, the next informative question is encoding sensitivity under controlled base-case equivalence.
\item We draw an audit-oriented corollary rather than a full characterization theorem: trustworthy finite witness evidence can certify that a boundary is real, but canonical repair presentation still depends on an explicit abstraction contract.
\end{itemize}
