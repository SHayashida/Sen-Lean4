\section{Contributions}
\label{sec:contributions}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item We distinguish raw repair explanation from grouped repair explanation; the former is experimentally shown to be non-invariant under clause-equivalent encodings, while the latter requires an explicit abstraction contract.

\item We separate finite witness legitimization from repair presentation canonicality: the former concerns whether a finite impossibility witness can be trusted at all, while the latter concerns whether and how its repairs can be canonically compared across encodings. Theorem~\ref{thm:non-invariance} shows that success on the first question does not settle the second, even in the presence of clause-multiset equivalence.

\item We clarify the methodological role of the Candidate~A and Candidate~B comparison within the repository's experimental trajectory: Candidate~A showed that the current five-lever granularity collapses neighboring-case repair tests into trivial singleton repairs, motivating the pivot to Candidate~B, which exhibits non-invariance at the encoding-sensitivity level under controlled base-case clause equivalence.
\end{itemize}
