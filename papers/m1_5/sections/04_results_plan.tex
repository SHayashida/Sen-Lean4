\section{Main Theorem \& Witness}

The grounding artifact bundle for this section is
\code{results/20260401/candidate\_b\_minlib\_granularity/}.
Within that bundle, the main witness is the paired comparison between the bundled witness
\code{asymm+un+minlib+no\_cycle4}
and the split witness
\code{asymm+un+decisive\_voter0+decisive\_voter1+no\_cycle4}.
All claims below remain within the current local-rationality setting and the Sen24 base case.

\paragraph{Theorem 1 (Non-invariance of Raw Repair Explanation).}
There exist lever-structured encodings \((\Lambda_b,\mathcal{S}_b,\varphi_b)\) and \((\Lambda_s,\mathcal{S}_s,\varphi_s)\), together with active lever sets \(S_b \in \mathcal{S}_b\) and \(S_s \in \mathcal{S}_s\), such that
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*),leftmargin=1.8em]
\item \(\varphi_b(S_b) \equiv_{\mathsf{CM}} \varphi_s(S_s)\);
\item both \(\varphi_b(S_b)\) and \(\varphi_s(S_s)\) are \(\mathsf{UNSAT}\);
\item
\[
\mathsf{Rep}^{\mathrm{raw}}_{\Lambda_b}(\varphi_b(S_b))
\not\equiv_{\mathsf{RRE}}
\mathsf{Rep}^{\mathrm{raw}}_{\Lambda_s}(\varphi_s(S_s)).
\]
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Proof.}
We use the explicit Candidate B witness pair recorded in
\code{results/20260401/candidate\_b\_minlib\_granularity/summary.md}
and \code{comparison.json}.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{Step \arabic*.},leftmargin=2.2em]
\item
Define \(\Lambda_b\) to be the bundled five-lever family consisting of
\code{asymm}, \code{un}, \code{minlib}, \code{no\_cycle3}, and \code{no\_cycle4},
and let \(S_b\) activate \code{asymm}, \code{un}, \code{minlib}, and \code{no\_cycle4}.
Define \(\Lambda_s\) to be the split six-lever family consisting of
\code{asymm}, \code{un}, \code{decisive\_voter0}, \code{decisive\_voter1}, \code{no\_cycle3}, and \code{no\_cycle4},
and let \(S_s\) activate \code{asymm}, \code{un}, \code{decisive\_voter0}, \code{decisive\_voter1}, and \code{no\_cycle4}.
Let \(\varphi_b\) and \(\varphi_s\) denote the corresponding repository encodings for the bundled and split families.

\item
The mapped comparison artifact reports that this bundled/split witness pair is clause-multiset equivalent under the designated comparison map and that the mapped bundled and split statuses agree.
Hence, for this witness pair,
\[
\varphi_b(S_b) \equiv_{\mathsf{CM}} \varphi_s(S_s),
\]
and both encoded instances are \(\mathsf{UNSAT}\).
This uses the mapped comparison artifact directly and does not require any appeal to a stronger semantic equivalence claim.

\item
Write \(a,u,m,d_0,d_1,c_4\) for the levers
\code{asymm}, \code{un}, \code{minlib}, \code{decisive\_voter0}, \code{decisive\_voter1}, and \code{no\_cycle4}, respectively.
The artifact summary reports the following raw lever-level minimal repair families:
\[
\mathsf{Rep}^{\mathrm{raw}}_{\Lambda_b}(\varphi_b(S_b))
=
\bigl\{\{a\},\{u\},\{m\},\{c_4\}\bigr\},
\]
\[
\mathsf{Rep}^{\mathrm{raw}}_{\Lambda_s}(\varphi_s(S_s))
=
\bigl\{\{a\},\{u\},\{d_0\},\{d_1\},\{c_4\}\bigr\}.
\]
In particular, the bundled side contains the singleton repair \(\{m\}\), while the split side contains the person-specific singleton repairs \(\{d_0\}\) and \(\{d_1\}\).

\item
By Definition~4, \(\equiv_{\mathsf{RRE}}\) requires a bijection between the active lever sets.
But \(|S_b|=4\) and \(|S_s|=5\), so no bijection \(S_b \to S_s\) exists.
Equivalently, the two raw repair families contain different numbers of singleton repairs.
Therefore
\[
\mathsf{Rep}^{\mathrm{raw}}_{\Lambda_b}(\varphi_b(S_b))
\not\equiv_{\mathsf{RRE}}
\mathsf{Rep}^{\mathrm{raw}}_{\Lambda_s}(\varphi_s(S_s)).
\]
The three required conditions are satisfied, so the claimed existence result follows.
\end{enumerate}
\hfill\(\square\)

\paragraph{Witness interpretation.}
The theorem is intentionally narrow.
It says only that such a witness exists in the Sen24 base case and within the current local-rationality setting.
It does not say that every clause-equivalent encoding must yield different raw repairs, nor does it characterize the full space of grouped repair invariance.

\paragraph{Remark on the mapped comparison.}
The comparison artifact also reports clause-multiset equality and status preservation across all \(32\) mapped bundled-to-split base-case packages.
That broader result strengthens confidence in the repository comparison, but the proof above needs only the explicit witness pair.

\paragraph{Corollary 1 (Abstraction-contract corollary).}
Finite witness legitimization does not automatically determine a canonical repair presentation.
Therefore, audit-oriented repair presentation cannot proceed without an explicit choice of abstraction contract.

\paragraph{Discussion.}
The corollary is about presentation discipline, not about weakening the underlying impossibility witness.
The theorem shows that a proof-carrying or audit-carrying finite boundary can be stable while its raw repair explanation still changes with lever granularity.
Any grouped repair presentation that merges \code{decisive\_voter0} and \code{decisive\_voter1} into a single liberalism-level report is therefore an additional modeling choice, not a fact forced by the finite witness alone.
