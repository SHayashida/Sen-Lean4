\begin{abstract}
When a finite impossibility witness in social choice theory is generalised to a case family, ``the witness transferred'' is not one claim but two: a Proof-layer transfer of the semantic argument, and a Witness/Audit-layer transfer of the CNF encoding that backed the original certificate, and they come apart.
We demonstrate this layer split for Sen's Liberal Paradox by pairing a kernel-checked positive answer at the Lean Proof layer with an explicit scope wall against the CNF Witness/Audit layer.
Finite impossibility witnesses in social choice theory are increasingly delivered as proof-carrying artifacts: a kernel-checked Lean theorem at a base case, plus a CNF certificate audited at the same base case.
Concretely, we formalise and verify
\(\mathtt{sen\_impossibility\_lifts}\):
for any finite case with at least two voters and at least four alternatives, any social welfare function satisfying semantic unanimity (\(\mathtt{UN}\)) and minimal liberalism (\(\mathtt{MINLIB}\)) fails \(\mathtt{SocialAcyclic}\).
The proof ports the Sen24 base-case case-split (two-overlap, one-overlap, disjoint) at the semantic axiom level and does not consume the hypothesis \(\mathtt{\_hm : 4 \leq m}\); the overlap case analysis avoids requiring a universal four-point completion in \(\mathtt{Fin\ m}\).
On the negative side, the Lean lift does not automatically upgrade the sen24 CNF atlas into a family-level CNF certificate.
The CNF Witness/Audit layer certifies only the sen24-audited short-cycle encoding \(\mathtt{no\_cycle3 \wedge no\_cycle4}\), whose equivalence to full acyclicity was audited only at four alternatives.
For \(m \geq 5\) that equivalence does not transfer automatically.
The contribution is therefore a Transfer Contract: proof-level transfer is kernel-checked and reaches full acyclicity, while witness-level transfer requires a separately audited encoding contract.
\end{abstract}
