\begin{abstract}
Finite impossibility witnesses in social choice theory are increasingly delivered as proof-carrying artifacts: a kernel-checked Lean theorem at a base case, plus a CNF certificate audited at the same base case.
When such a witness is generalized to a case family, ``the witness transferred'' is not one claim but two---a Proof-layer transfer of the semantic argument, and a Witness/Audit-layer transfer of the CNF encoding that backed the original certificate---and the two come apart.
We demonstrate this layer split for Sen's Liberal Paradox through a kernel-checked finite semantic obstruction basis.
At the Lean Proof layer, semantic \(\mathtt{UN}\) and \(\mathtt{MINLIB}\) produce one of three bounded semantic obstruction shapes---O2, O3, or O4---with exact support cardinalities \(2,3,4\).
These shapes respectively yield a strict conflict, a 3-cycle, or a 4-cycle and therefore refute \(\mathtt{SocialAcyclic}\).
The generic bridge theorem is stated for an arbitrary voter type and a finite alternative type; the existing \(\mathtt{sen\_impossibility\_lifts}\) theorem over \((\mathtt{Fin}\ n,\mathtt{Fin}\ m)\) is retained as a compatibility corollary.
Here ``basis'' means a complete finite generating family of semantic obstruction shapes, not a minimal or irredundant basis.
On the negative side, the Lean bridge does not automatically upgrade the sen24 CNF certificate into a family-level one: the Witness/Audit layer certifies only the sen24-audited short-cycle encoding \(\mathtt{no\_cycle3 \wedge no\_cycle4}\), whose equivalence to full acyclicity was audited only at four alternatives, and for \(m \geq 5\) that equivalence does not transfer automatically.
The contribution is therefore a Transfer Contract: proof-level semantic transfer is kernel-checked, while witness-level transfer requires a separately audited encoding contract.
\end{abstract}
