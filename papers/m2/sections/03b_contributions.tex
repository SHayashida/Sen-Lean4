\section{Contributions}
\label{sec:contributions}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item We expose \(\mathtt{MINLIB}\) as a reusable semantic witness interface.
  The Lean structure \(\mathtt{SenRightsWitness}\) packages two distinguished rights holders, two decisive ordered pairs, and the at-most-four distinguished alternatives carried by those pairs.

\item We prove an exhaustive O2/O3/O4 overlap classification.
  Every extracted witness is classified by the overlap of \(\{x,y\}\) and \(\{z,w\}\), producing the finite canonical support family O2, O3, and O4.

\item We prove exact support cardinalities.
  O2 has support cardinality \(2\), O3 has support cardinality \(3\), and O4 has support cardinality \(4\).
  These are kernel-checked in \(\mathtt{SocialChoiceAtlas/Sen/ObstructionSoundness.lean}\), not merely described in prose.

\item We prove shape-specific soundness.
  O2 yields a strict social conflict, O3 yields a 3-cycle, and O4 yields a 4-cycle.
  Each is constructed using an ambient profile with at most two ranking types; this is not a literal two-voter restriction theorem.

\item We prove the generic impossibility theorem.
  The public theorem \(\mathtt{sen\_outcome\_obstruction\_of\_UN\_MINLIB}\) produces a semantic obstruction from \(\mathtt{UN}\) and \(\mathtt{MINLIB}\), and \(\mathtt{sen\_impossibility\_from\_obstruction\_basis}\) refutes \(\mathtt{SocialAcyclic}\).
  The generic theorem uses an arbitrary voter type and a finite decidable alternative type; it does not require voter finiteness or explicit lower-bound hypotheses.

\item We retain the legacy \((\mathtt{Fin}\ n,\mathtt{Fin}\ m)\) theorem as a compatibility corollary.
  The theorem \(\mathtt{sen\_impossibility\_lifts}\) keeps its public name and statement shape, including \(\mathtt{\_hn}\) and \(\mathtt{\_hm}\), but its proof body now specializes the generic obstruction-basis theorem.

\item We keep the Proof-vs-Witness/Audit scope wall unchanged.
  The Lean bridge establishes semantic proof transfer to full acyclicity, but it does not automatically upgrade the Sen24 CNF atlas, LRAT certificate, short-cycle audit, or repair family into family-level artifacts.
  This is the contract body, not an appendix-level caveat: a complete Transfer Contract must record both sides of the boundary.

\item We separate this paper's scope from a deferred representation-canonicity question.
  The representation-non-canonicity phenomenon---whether the lever-level repair presentation under which repairs are reported transfers canonically at family scale---is real but belongs to M2.1.
  Accordingly we defer it and treat it here only as a forward pointer (Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}).
\end{itemize}
