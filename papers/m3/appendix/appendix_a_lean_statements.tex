\section{Compiled Lean Theorem Statements}
\label{app:lean}

The following signatures are copied from the compiled modules under
\code{SocialChoiceAtlas/Reportability/}.  Namespace qualification and proof
bodies are omitted; hypotheses and conclusions are unchanged.

The reportability modules are the kernel-checked theorem core of this paper.
At this revision, \code{lake build} passes for the repository, possibly with
existing non-fatal warnings, and the main declarations listed below are
closed without \code{sorry}.  The formalization is intentionally abstract: it
proves finite-set reportability theorems and does not formalize CNF generation,
solver execution, or the Candidate B artifact enumeration.

\subsection{Core definition: \code{PsiDeletionMonotonicity}}
\begin{Verbatim}[fontsize=\scriptsize]
def PsiDeletionMonotonicity (I : Finset Atom)
    (SatPsi : Finset Atom → Prop) : Prop :=
  ∀ T' T, T' ⊆ T → T ⊆ I → SatPsi T → SatPsi T'
\end{Verbatim}

This is the retained-set convention.  The premise \(T' \subseteq T\) means
that the residual retaining \(T'\) deletes at least as many contract atoms as
the residual retaining \(T\).  The hypothesis \(T \subseteq I\), together with
\(T' \subseteq T\), implies \(T' \subseteq I\); this is why the Lean definition
only needs the explicit \(T \subseteq I\) premise.  This matches Definition~5
in the main text.
