\section{Arrow Scope Boundary}
\label{app:arrow}

Arrow is recorded as a prerequisite testbed for truth/certificate scope, not as
a second M3 instantiation.

\paragraph{Finite atlas facts.}
A status established for one finite Arrow encoding may be consumed by M3 only
as a residual in that declared finite scope.  Its artifact and audit obligations
remain local to that encoding.

\paragraph{Family-level claims.}
A claim across a finite family requires transfer legitimacy for every residual
that the reportability contract may query.  A theorem-specific transfer of the
fully active UNSAT apex does not establish the proper SAT residuals.

\paragraph{Unrestricted Arrow claims.}
An unrestricted theorem requires a separate mathematical or formal transfer
result.  M3 supplies no such bridge and must not be cited as one.

\paragraph{Per-residual obligation.}
For each retained atom set \(T\subseteq I\), the reference residual
\(\SatPsi(T)\) must be justified at the claimed scope.  SAT residuals require a
uniform witness construction or an explicit finite-only restriction.  If this
obligation is unresolved, the associated reportability conclusion stops at the
finite artifact-defined contract.
