\begin{abstract}
Auditable finite-atlas impossibility evidence can show that a residual problem
is infeasible, but it does not by itself determine how repairs should be
reported.  In finite social-choice encodings, raw implementation-level repairs
may be representation-sensitive: clause-equivalent bundled and split
realizations can have different inclusion-minimal lever deletions.  This paper
introduces reportability contracts, a finite-set interface for separating raw
repairs from grouped contract-level reports.  M3-A gives a syntactic sufficient
condition: atomic implementation blocks plus residual faithfulness make grouped
reports coincide with contract repairs and support atom-indexed raw transport.
M3-B covers non-atomic interfaces by replacing atomicity with
\(\GroupSoundness\), requiring every feasible implementation deletion to remain
feasible after grouping.  M3-C gives a characterization in deletion-monotone
reference contracts: for residually faithful realizations, grouped correctness
is equivalent to \(\GroupSoundness\).  The theorem core is mechanized in Lean
as abstract finite-set theorems, independent of CNF solving.  The Candidate B
audit gives an artifact-backed, artifact-defined instantiation: the witness
remains raw-noncanonical, while grouped canonicity is justified only relative
to the recorded bundled contract, without upgrading the artifact to semantic
or Lean-level validity.
\end{abstract}
