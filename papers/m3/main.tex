\begin{abstract}
Auditable finite-atlas impossibility evidence can show that a residual problem
is infeasible, but it does not by itself determine how repairs should be
reported.  In finite social-choice encodings, semantic axioms such as
liberalism may be split into several implementation levers, so raw
implementation-level repairs can be representation-sensitive even when the
certificate evidence is trusted.  This paper introduces reportability
contracts, a finite-set interface for separating raw repairs from grouped
contract-level reports.  M3-A gives a syntactic sufficient condition: atomic
implementation blocks plus residual faithfulness make grouped reports coincide
with contract repairs and support atom-indexed raw transport.  M3-B covers
non-atomic interfaces by replacing atomicity with \(\GroupSoundness\).  M3-C
characterizes the deletion-monotone regime: for residually faithful
realizations, grouped correctness is equivalent to \(\GroupSoundness\).  The
theorem core is mechanized in Lean as abstract finite-set theorems, independent
of CNF solving.  In Candidate B, the split encoding exposes two distinct
person-indexed singleton repairs; under the declared artifact-defined bundled
contract, M3-B makes their grouped repair predicate coincide pointwise with the
contract repair predicate.  Thus \(\mathtt{minlib}\) is a report-correct
grouped label under this contract, not the unique or normatively preferred
repair, and not a semantic or Lean-level upgrade of the artifact.
\end{abstract}
