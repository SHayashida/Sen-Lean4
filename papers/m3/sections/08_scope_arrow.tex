\section{Scope and the Arrow Boundary}
\label{sec:arrow-scope}

Arrow is a truth/certificate prerequisite before repair/report comparison.
It is not a second example of the present result.

Sen24 is sufficient as this paper's separating witness for the distinction
between the atomic and non-atomic reportability routes.  Arrow remains a
separate generality and transfer test rather than a prerequisite for the
present theorem.

This paper does not supply finite-to-general transfer.  Its theorems consume a declared
reference residual predicate over the complete contract deletion lattice.
Before an Arrow residual can be used at family scale, that residual needs its
own transfer legitimacy.

The fully active UNSAT apex and the proper SAT residuals have different proof
obligations.  A theorem-specific impossibility transfer may legitimize the
apex, while each SAT residual intended for family-level use needs a uniform rule
construction or an explicit finite-scope restriction.  Apex transfer cannot be
silently extended to the residual lattice.

If Arrow remains finite-only, that is not a failure of the present result.  It is evidence that
the reportability layer is respecting the certified scope of its reference
problems.  Appendix~\ref{app:arrow} records the finite, family-level, and
unrestricted claim classes without adding a new Arrow theorem.
