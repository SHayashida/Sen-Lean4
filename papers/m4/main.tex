\title{Auditable Claim Boundaries for Finite Social-Choice Artifacts:\\
A Sen24 Case Study}
\author{Shunya Hayashida}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Formal artifacts can certify that a decision rule is impossible, inconsistent,
or repairable. But they do not automatically determine which repair or
reporting claim is licensed by the evidence. This preprint studies that issue
in the finite Sen24 setting, treating a small social-choice artifact as a case
study in disciplined claim reporting. Under the declared M4 encoding, it
records a finite audit of the repair and reporting structure, including the
cell classification, repair classification, and grouped-report checks
documented in the M4 claim boundary. It also identifies one central semantic
condition and connects it to a Lean-backed target, with a sufficient bridge
from two fixed conditions to \code{MINLIB}. The paper deliberately separates
those supported statements from future artifact-correctness obligations,
including Python encoder correctness, generated-clause correctness, CNF
artifact correctness, and full semantic-to-CNF correctness. Its contribution is
a claim-boundary methodology for finite social-choice artifacts, not an
end-to-end verification result or a new general theorem of Sen.
\end{abstract}
