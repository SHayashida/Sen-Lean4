\section{Conclusion}

We provide a small-scope template for auditable possibility atlases in computational social choice.
The sen24 study shows that small-scope exploration can still produce publishable automated-reasoning evidence when the chain of trust is explicit: the frontier is recorded as replayable artifacts, local UNSAT explanation remains limited to one MUS plus one small MCS candidate, stronger repair reasoning appears separately through full enumeration plus triangulation, committed UNSAT reference artifacts are kernel-checked from LRAT, reported SAT witnesses are validated against CNF, and the remaining encoder boundary is exposed as the audit contract of Proposition~\ref{prop:sen24-contract} rather than hidden inside solver trust.

Any later expansion of the atlas should preserve the same trust and reproducibility discipline.
